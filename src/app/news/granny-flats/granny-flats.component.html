San Diego County Waives Permit Fees

January 18th, 2019

The San Diego County Board of Supervisors voted unanimously in an attempt to fight the affordable housing crisis.

San Diego County Board of Supervisors voted unanimously in an attempt to fight the affordable housing crisis. The county will spend $11 million over five years. This trial program waives fees to encourage homeowners to build “granny flats” on their properties.

This program follows the State of California law that was passed in 2018. The state law eased restrictions and allows property owners to build more granny flats. The San Diego county law immediately waives building permit fees, onsite waste water fees and development impact fees, which include transportation fees to add roads and infrastructure, fees to build parks, and drainage fees; for homeowners who want to build granny flats.

The county's regulations allow property owner to build rental units up to 1,200 sq ft, and may be attached to or built separately from existing homes on the same parcel of land. They cannot be sold separately and must meet all zoning requirements. This is great for those property owners that have been considering a project like this. By waiving permit and development fees, owners can save a quite a bit of money.
