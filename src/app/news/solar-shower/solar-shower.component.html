Solar Shower

April 10th, 2019

Are you looking for a way to lower your impact on our environment? Use the power of the sun to generate your hot water.

This solar shower was built a few years ago using pallets and left over materials. The homeowner asked me to move it from it's current location, to a more practical one. After disassembling it and making a few improvements; they now have an outdoor shower with water hot enough that you need to temper it with cold water in the same way you would a conventional shower.

I coated the the exterior walls with Thompson's Water Seal - Semi Transparent Cedar Stain. And painted the deck with an exterior paint with primer. My friend Ashley painted the towel hooks, shelves, and benches with paint leftover from previous projects. After resetting everything, the homeowner allowed me to take it for a rest run, and it worked great.

Since you are still reading, here are the components of this shower.

  • Garden Hose
  • Black plastic pipe and a location to coil it
  • 1 - Garden Hose Y-fitting
  • Water Temperature Control with Shower Head
  • 4 Walls
  • Platform
  • Galvanized Trough
  • Drain Line (section of garden hose)

    • Improvements & Modifications

    The home owner asked me to move their solar shower inside their pool area. It was previously just outside that area and you were required to go through two gates to access it.

    After disassembling the water supply and the walls from the deck, I moved all the components to the new location, starting with the hot and cold water supply. The walls got a fresh coat of Thompson's, the deck got two coats of exterior paint with primer, and the shelves and towel hooks received two coats of exterior paint with primer and created a fourth wall, (something that was lacking due to it's previous location).

    How It Works

    An ordinary garden hose supplies the water; both to the hot and the cold. The Y-connection diverts water to the coil of black pipe that is heated by the sun and gives us our cold water. Recoiling this water line was a challenge; as I am sure it was originally.

    When you buy a coil of hose, whether it be a garden hose, PEX, or something else, the manufacturer coils it for ease of shipping and display in the store. After weeks and months of being rolled up, these products retain that shape. We usually need them to conform to a different shape, and you should plan accordingly. Laying them out in the sun and or filling them with water will help them relax and make it that much easier to handle.

    As expected, the sun heats the water in the coil of pipe. Each water line is connected to the shower head (reclaimed kitchen sink faucet and shower head). The water used falls into a galvanized trough, that is outfitted with a valve and connected to a garden hose. The trough collects the water and the garden hose diverts the water out to the edge of an embankment, where it is free to seep back into the soil.
