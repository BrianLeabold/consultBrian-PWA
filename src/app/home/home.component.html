Grandson of a carpenter Son of a remodeler

I started swinging a hammer the summer after I turned 12. Nailing down plywood sub-floor by hand, side by side with my grand-dad and my father, is a fond memory that has fueled my love of construction all my life. Through out high school I worked for my dad on the weekends and over summer vacations. He taught me about dealing with other trades, suppliers and our own customers.

Then in September of my senior year of high school, I decided to join the Navy. Building on what I learned working for my father, I learned more about people and their plethora of personalities. I was able to see places and things that has fueled my ability to envision the big picture, yet focus on important details that make my projects feel complete.
